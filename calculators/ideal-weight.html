Understanding Ideal Weight

📏 Multiple Formulas

We use Robinson, Miller, and Hamwi formulas developed by medical professionals to provide a comprehensive weight range rather than a single number.

🏗️ Frame Size Matters

Body frame size significantly affects ideal weight. Large frames can healthily carry 10% more weight, while small frames may be healthier at 10% less.

🎯 Health Focus

Ideal weight is about health, not appearance. Consider muscle mass, bone density, and overall fitness rather than just the number on the scale.

Ideal Weight FAQ

How do I determine my body frame size?
Measure your wrist circumference. For women: <6.25" = small, 6.25-6.5" = medium, >6.5" = large. For men: <6.5" = small, 6.5-7.5" = medium, >7.5" = large.
Why do the formulas give different results?
Different formulas were developed for different populations and purposes. The range gives you a better understanding than a single number, as ideal weight varies individually.
Should I aim for the lowest weight in my range?
Not necessarily. Aim for the middle of your range initially. Athletes with more muscle mass may be healthier at the higher end, while those with less muscle may prefer the lower end.
How does age affect ideal weight?
Metabolism slows with age, and muscle mass typically decreases. Some formulas account for this, suggesting slightly higher weights may be healthy for older adults.
