EMI Calculator

How EMI Calculator Works

An EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is fully paid off.

Our EMI calculator helps you determine your monthly payment for any loan, whether it's a home loan, personal loan, car loan, or any other type of installment loan. Understanding your EMI helps you plan your finances better and choose loan terms that fit your budget.

EMI Calculation Formula

The EMI is calculated using the following mathematical formula:


EMI = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ / ((1 + r)ⁿ - 1)

Where:
P = Principal loan amount
r = Monthly interest rate (Annual rate / 12 / 100)
n = Number of monthly installments (Years × 12)

Component Breakdown

  • Principal: The original loan amount borrowed
  • Interest Rate: Annual percentage rate charged by lender
  • Tenure: Total repayment period in years
  • Total Interest: Total amount paid as interest over loan tenure
  • Total Amount: Principal + Total Interest

Frequently Asked Questions

What factors affect my EMI amount?
Three main factors affect your EMI: the loan amount (principal), interest rate, and loan tenure. Higher loan amounts and interest rates increase EMI, while longer tenure reduces monthly EMI but increases total interest paid.
How can I reduce my EMI burden?
You can reduce EMI by: making a higher down payment to reduce principal, negotiating for lower interest rates, choosing longer tenure (but this increases total interest), or making prepayments when possible.
What happens if I miss an EMI payment?
Missing EMI payments can result in late fees, penalty charges, negative impact on credit score, and in severe cases, legal action or asset seizure. Always contact your lender if you're facing payment difficulties.
Is it better to choose shorter or longer loan tenure?
Shorter tenure means higher EMI but lower total interest paid. Longer tenure means lower EMI but higher total interest. Choose based on your monthly budget and financial goals.
Can I prepay my loan to reduce EMI or tenure?
Yes, most lenders allow prepayments. You can either reduce your EMI amount or loan tenure. Prepayments directly reduce the principal, saving significant interest over time.
