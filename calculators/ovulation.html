Understanding Your Cycle

A typical menstrual cycle has four phases:

PhaseDays (28-day cycle)What Happens
MenstruationDays 1–5Uterine lining sheds
FollicularDays 1–13Follicles develop; estrogen rises
OvulationDay 14Egg is released — peak fertility
LutealDays 15–28Progesterone rises; uterus prepares

Frequently Asked Questions

When do I ovulate?
Ovulation usually occurs ~14 days before your next period. For a 28-day cycle, that's day 14. The fertile window is the 5 days before ovulation plus ovulation day.
How long is the fertile window?
About 6 days: 5 days before ovulation + ovulation day. Sperm can survive 3–5 days; the egg is viable 12–24 hours. The 2–3 days before ovulation are most fertile.
What are signs of ovulation?
Clear stretchy mucus (egg-white texture), mild pelvic cramps, slight temperature rise, increased libido, and breast tenderness are common signs.
Is this calculator accurate?
It provides estimates based on average cycle patterns. Most accurate for regular cycles. Irregular cycles make prediction harder. For fertility treatment, always work with a healthcare provider.
