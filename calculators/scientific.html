Scientific Calculator FAQ

How do I use trigonometric functions?
Click the trig function (sin, cos, tan), enter your angle, then press equals. Make sure you're in the correct angle mode (degrees or radians) for your calculation.
What's the difference between log and ln?
Log is the common logarithm (base 10), while ln is the natural logarithm (base e). Use log for most engineering calculations and ln for advanced mathematics.
How do I calculate powers and roots?
Use x^y for any power, x² for squares, x³ for cubes, or √ for square roots. For other roots, use x^(1/n) where n is the root.
Can I use parentheses for complex expressions?
Yes! Use parentheses to group operations and ensure correct order of calculation. The calculator follows proper mathematical precedence rules.
