Sales Tax FAQ

How is sales tax calculated?
Sales tax is calculated by multiplying the pre-tax price by the tax rate. For example: $100 × 8.25% = $8.25 tax, for a total of $108.25.
Why do tax rates vary by location?
Sales tax rates are set by state, county, and local governments. Some states have no sales tax, while others combine state, county, and city taxes reaching 10%+ in some areas.
What items are typically tax-exempt?
Common tax-exempt items include groceries, prescription medications, and clothing (in some states). Tax exemptions vary significantly by state and locality.
How do I find my local tax rate?
Check your local government websites, recent receipts, or tax authority websites. Remember that your rate may include state, county, and city taxes combined.
