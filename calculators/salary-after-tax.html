Understanding Salary After Tax Calculations

Your take-home pay is significantly different from your gross salary due to various tax deductions. Understanding these deductions helps you budget effectively, negotiate salaries, and plan for major purchases. Our calculator provides estimates based on current federal tax brackets and typical state tax rates.

The calculation includes federal income tax (progressive brackets), state income tax (varies by state), FICA taxes (Social Security and Medicare), and accounts for pre-tax deductions that can reduce your taxable income. These estimates help you understand your actual purchasing power and plan your finances.

Tax Deduction Components

Federal Income Tax

Progressive tax system based on 2024 tax brackets:

  • 10% on income up to $11,000 (single) / $22,000 (married filing jointly)
  • 12% on income $11,001-$44,725 (single) / $22,001-$89,450 (married filing jointly)
  • 22% on income $44,726-$95,375 (single) / $89,451-$190,750 (married filing jointly)
  • 24% on income $95,376-$182,050 (single) / $190,751-$364,200 (married filing jointly)
  • Higher brackets apply to higher income levels

FICA Taxes (Payroll Taxes)


Social Security Tax: 6.2% (on wages up to $160,200 in 2024)
Medicare Tax: 1.45% (on all wages)
Additional Medicare Tax: 0.9% (on wages over $200,000 single / $250,000 married)
Total FICA: 7.65% for most earners

State Income Tax

Varies by state:

  • No State Tax: Alaska, Florida, Nevada, New Hampshire, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, Wyoming
  • Low Tax (0-5%): States like North Carolina, Utah, Colorado
  • Medium Tax (5-8%): States like Virginia, Georgia, Kansas
  • High Tax (8%+): States like California, New York, Hawaii

Pre-tax Deductions

Reduce taxable income:

  • 401(k) contributions (up to $23,000 in 2024)
  • Health insurance premiums
  • HSA contributions (up to $4,150 individual / $8,300 family in 2024)
  • Dental and vision insurance
  • Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this salary calculator?
This calculator provides estimates based on general tax brackets and standard deductions. Actual taxes may vary due to specific deductions, credits, state laws, local taxes, and other factors. Consult a tax professional for precise calculations.
What taxes are included in the calculation?
The calculator includes federal income tax, state income tax (if applicable), Social Security tax (6.2%), Medicare tax (1.45%), and additional Medicare tax for high earners (0.9% over threshold). It doesn't include local taxes, disability insurance, or union dues.
Why is my actual paycheck different from this calculation?
Actual paychecks may differ due to local taxes, state disability insurance, union dues, voluntary deductions, different withholding elections, or employer-specific benefits. This calculator provides federal and state estimates only.
Should I adjust my tax withholdings?
If you consistently get large refunds, you may be over-withholding and giving the government an interest-free loan. If you owe money at tax time, you may be under-withholding. Use IRS Form W-4 and their withholding calculator to adjust appropriately.
How do bonuses and overtime affect take-home pay?
Bonuses are often withheld at a flat 22% federal rate (supplemental wage rate), which may result in over or under-withholding depending on your actual tax bracket. Overtime is taxed as regular income but may push you into higher brackets.
