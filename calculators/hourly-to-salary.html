How to Convert Hourly to Annual Salary

Converting an hourly wage to an annual salary is straightforward. The standard formula assumes a 40-hour work week and 52 weeks per year:

Annual Salary = Hourly Rate × Hours per Week × Weeks per Year

For a typical full-time employee working 40 hours/week for 52 weeks:

  • Annual: Hourly Rate × 2,080
  • Monthly: Annual ÷ 12
  • Weekly: Hourly Rate × Hours per Week
  • Daily: Hourly Rate × Hours per Day (typically 8)

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert hourly wage to annual salary?
Multiply your hourly wage × hours per week × 52 weeks. At 40 hrs/week: Annual = Hourly × 2,080. Example: $20/hr × 2,080 = $41,600/year.
How many hours a year is full-time?
A standard full-time job is 40 hours/week × 52 weeks = 2,080 hours per year. This doesn't account for paid time off or holidays.
What is $15 an hour annually?
$15/hr × 40 hrs × 52 weeks = $31,200/year gross. That's $2,600/month, $600/week, or $120/day before taxes.
Does this include overtime pay?
No — this calculator uses straight-time pay at the hours you enter. To include overtime, add overtime hours separately at 1.5× your base rate.
Are these figures before or after taxes?
All figures shown are gross (before tax) earnings. Use our Salary After Tax Calculator to estimate your take-home pay.
