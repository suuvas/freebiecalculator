How to Convert Hourly Rate to Annual Salary

By FreebieCalculator Team June 9, 2026 8 min read

Whether you're comparing job offers, budgeting, or negotiating pay, knowing how to convert an hourly rate to an annual salary is an essential skill. The math is simple — and this guide walks you through it step by step.

The Basic Formula

The standard conversion assumes a 40-hour work week and 52 weeks per year:

Annual Salary = Hourly Rate × Hours per Week × Weeks per Year

For a full-time employee: Annual = Hourly Rate × 2,080 (because 40 × 52 = 2,080 total hours/year).

Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1: $20/hour full-time

  • Annual: $20 × 40 × 52 = $41,600
  • Monthly: $41,600 ÷ 12 = $3,466.67
  • Bi-weekly: $20 × 80 = $1,600
  • Weekly: $20 × 40 = $800
  • Daily: $20 × 8 = $160

Example 2: $15/hour part-time (30 hrs/week)

  • Annual: $15 × 30 × 52 = $23,400
  • Monthly: $23,400 ÷ 12 = $1,950

What About Taxes?

These figures are gross pay — before taxes. Your actual take-home pay will be lower after federal income tax (10–37%), state income tax (0–13%), Social Security (6.2%), and Medicare (1.45%).

For a rough estimate: most workers take home 65–75% of gross pay. So $20/hour gross ≈ $27,000–$31,000 take-home annually.

Use our Salary After Tax Calculator or Paycheck Calculator for an accurate net pay estimate.

Reverse: Converting Salary to Hourly

Want to go the other way? Divide your annual salary by 2,080 (full-time hours):

Hourly Rate = Annual Salary ÷ 2,080

Example: $65,000 ÷ 2,080 = $31.25/hour

This is useful when comparing salaried positions against hourly job offers.
